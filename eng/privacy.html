Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how HealthMaster ("HealthMaster", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information when you use the HealthMaster nutrition tracking and AI coaching service. HealthMaster is delivered through two channels: a Telegram bot (@calai_bot) and a native Android application distributed via Google Play. The same service is also available in a web interface and, where indicated, on iOS via the App Store. This Policy applies to all of these surfaces.

1. Introduction

HealthMaster helps people log meals, track calories and macronutrients (protein, fat, carbohydrates), monitor weight and body-composition goals, and receive AI-generated nutrition coaching. To do this, the service processes the meal descriptions, photos, and voice messages you choose to share with it, along with the account information needed to keep your data tied to you across devices and sessions.

We aim to collect only what is necessary to operate the service, to be transparent about every third party that touches your data, and to give you full control over deletion. If anything in this Policy is unclear, write to support@healthmaster.app and we will explain.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Account Data

  • Telegram user ID and basic Telegram profile fields (first name, language code, username if set) when you use the bot.
  • Email address when you sign in to the Android or web app via email or via Google Sign-In / Apple Sign-In.
  • Display name and profile picture, only if you provide them or if returned by Google / Apple / Telegram.
  • Authentication provider identifier (Google sub, Apple user ID, Telegram ID, or email) used to link your account across surfaces.
  • Account creation date, last login timestamp, and current subscription tier.

2.2 Health and Nutrition Data

  • Meal entries: free-text descriptions, recognised dish names, portion sizes, calories, protein, fat, carbohydrate values, fibre, and meal timestamps.
  • Food photos you upload to be recognised by AI.
  • Voice recordings you send describing what you ate.
  • Body metrics you choose to log: weight, height, age, sex, target weight, activity level, dietary preferences, allergies, and personal goals.
  • Daily nutrition targets calculated for you (BMR, TDEE, macro split).
  • Conversations with the AI coach inside the app or bot.

2.3 Device and Technical Data

  • Android version, application version, device model, and screen locale — collected with each request to identify compatibility issues.
  • An anonymous device identifier generated by the app and stored locally; sent to our servers only at the moment of an in-app purchase (Google Play Billing) to associate the purchase with your account and prevent fraud.
  • IP address and approximate region — derived from network metadata for security logging, abuse prevention, and fraud detection. Logs containing IP are retained for 30 days.
  • Crash reports and non-fatal error traces (excluding personally identifiable content).

2.4 Usage Data

  • In-app interactions such as which screens are opened, which buttons are tapped, and the timing of feature use — aggregated and pseudonymised.
  • Number of meals logged, AI coach messages sent, and subscription status, used to operate the service and produce aggregate analytics.

We do not collect: precise GPS location, contacts, SMS messages, calendar, call logs, browsing history outside the app, or biometric identifiers (no fingerprint, face, or voiceprint identification).

3. Sensitive Permissions on Android

The Android application requests the following runtime permissions. Each permission is requested only when the relevant feature is first used, can be revoked at any time in system settings, and is used strictly for the purpose described below. We do not collect data from these permissions for any purpose other than the feature requested.

Permission Why we request it If you decline
CAMERA To take a photo of a meal so the AI can recognise the dish and estimate calories and macros. Photos taken in-app are uploaded to our backend and forwarded to OpenAI for recognition. They are not used for advertising, profiling, training third-party models, or any other purpose. The "Photo recognition" feature is disabled. All other functions (text logging, voice logging, manual entry, AI coach) continue to work.
RECORD_AUDIO (microphone) To record a short voice description of a meal, which is transcribed and parsed by the AI. Recordings are uploaded to our backend and forwarded to OpenAI for transcription. They are not used for voiceprint identification, training third-party models, or advertising. The "Voice logging" feature is disabled. Text and photo logging continue to work.
READ_MEDIA_IMAGES (Android 13+) / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (Android 12 and below) To let you pick an existing photo of a meal from your gallery instead of taking a new one. The app reads only the image you select. You can still take new photos with the camera or log meals as text or voice. The gallery picker is hidden.
POST_NOTIFICATIONS (Android 13+) To send local reminders to log meals and weekly progress summaries. Notifications are scheduled locally on the device and contain no third-party advertising. You will not receive reminders. All other features work normally.
INTERNET, ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE Required to communicate with our backend and to detect whether the device is online. The app cannot function offline beyond viewing previously cached entries.
com.android.vending.BILLING Required by Google Play to process in-app subscription purchases. We do not see your card details; only the Google Play purchase token reaches our servers. You cannot start a paid subscription via Google Play, but the free tier remains available.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • To provide core service functionality: meal logging, calorie and macro calculation, weight tracking, and goal management.
  • To run AI-based food recognition on the photos and voice messages you submit, and to generate personalised nutrition recommendations.
  • To maintain your account across devices and let you continue your history when switching between Telegram, Android, web, and (where available) iOS.
  • To process and verify subscription purchases through Google Play Billing, Apple App Store, or YooKassa.
  • To send transactional notifications (subscription expiry, payment receipts) and, with your consent, optional reminders.
  • To detect fraud, abuse, automated scraping, and unauthorised access.
  • To investigate bug reports, fix crashes, and improve product quality based on aggregated, pseudonymised usage data.
  • To comply with applicable laws, court orders, and lawful requests from public authorities.

We do not use your data for behavioural advertising, do not sell it to data brokers, and do not share it with social networks for targeted-ad purposes.

5. Third-Party Services

We use a limited set of third-party processors to deliver the service. Each is named below with the purpose of the transfer, the legal basis, and the location of processing.

5.1 OpenAI, L.L.C. (United States)

Photos of meals, voice recordings, and text descriptions you submit are forwarded to OpenAI for food recognition, transcription, and AI coach responses, using the OpenAI API. OpenAI processes this data as a subprocessor on our behalf and, under their Enterprise / API Data Processing Addendum, does not use API inputs or outputs to train their models. Transfer to the United States is governed by the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Art. 46 GDPR) for users in the EEA, UK, and Switzerland. See openai.com/policies/privacy-policy.

5.2 Google LLC (United States and EEA)

We use Google Sign-In for authentication, Google Play Billing for Android in-app subscriptions, and Google Cloud Storage for backups and object storage of photos. Google acts as an independent controller for sign-in and billing data, and as a processor for Cloud Storage. See policies.google.com/privacy.

5.3 Apple Inc. (United States and Ireland)

Where the iOS version is available, we use Sign in with Apple for authentication and the App Store for subscription billing. Apple processes this data under its own privacy policy. See apple.com/legal/privacy.

5.4 Telegram FZ-LLC (United Arab Emirates)

When you use the Telegram bot, messages flow through Telegram's infrastructure subject to Telegram's Privacy Policy. We receive only the data the Telegram Bot API exposes to bot operators (your Telegram ID, messages addressed to the bot, and the content you choose to send). See telegram.org/privacy.

5.5 YooKassa (NBCO YooMoney LLC, Russia) — legacy

For legacy Russian-region payment flows, payments are processed by YooKassa. We do not store full card numbers, CVV, or expiry dates; the payment provider supplies us only with a masked card identifier and a transaction status. New users outside the legacy region are processed via Google Play Billing or App Store instead.

5.6 Hosting and infrastructure

Backend services run on dedicated servers in the European Union (Hetzner, Germany). The marketing site is served by Netlify (United States) via Cloudflare. No personal account data flows through the marketing site.

6. Data Retention

  • Voice recordings: stored for up to 24 hours after upload, then automatically deleted. Only the resulting transcript and parsed meal entry are retained.
  • Meal photos: stored for up to 30 days so you can review and re-edit recent entries, then automatically deleted. The recognised text result and the meal entry remain.
  • Meal entries, weight history, goals, and AI conversations: retained until you delete the entry or your account.
  • Account profile: retained until you delete your account.
  • Payment records: retained for as long as required by accounting and tax laws of the operating jurisdiction (typically up to 5 years).
  • Server access logs and IP logs: retained for 30 days for security purposes.
  • Backups: rolling encrypted backups are retained for up to 30 days after deletion before being purged from cold storage.

7. Account Deletion

You can delete your account and the personal data tied to it at any time, by any of these methods:

  • From the Android app: Profile → Settings → Delete Account → Confirm.
  • From the Telegram bot: send the command /delete_account and confirm.
  • From the web: visit healthmaster.app/account-delete, sign in, and request deletion.
  • By email: write to support@healthmaster.app from the address linked to your account.

What gets deleted: your profile, meal history, weight history, photos and voice transcripts, AI conversations, and any pseudonymous identifiers tied to you. What may be retained: anonymised aggregate statistics that cannot be linked back to you, and payment records we are legally required to keep for tax and accounting purposes.

Timing: deletion from active systems is performed within 7 days of the request. Encrypted backups are purged on the rolling 30-day cycle, after which all copies are gone. If you ask for immediate purge of backups, we will do so where technically possible and confirm by email.

8. Children's Privacy

HealthMaster is not directed at children. The service is intended for users aged 13 and over, and in jurisdictions where the digital age of consent is higher (such as 16 in parts of the EU), the local minimum age applies. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children below the applicable age. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact support@healthmaster.app and we will delete it without delay. This Policy is consistent with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

9. International Data Transfers

Personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence, including the United States (OpenAI, Google, Apple) and the United Arab Emirates (Telegram). For users in the EEA, the United Kingdom, and Switzerland, these transfers are protected by the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses adopted under Article 46 GDPR, supplemented by technical measures (encryption in transit and at rest) and contractual restrictions on the use of the data by the receiving party.

10. Your Rights (GDPR — EEA, UK, Switzerland)

If you are located in the EEA, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access — to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — to have your data deleted, as described in section 7.
  • Right to restriction of processing — to limit how we use your data while a complaint is resolved.
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a machine-readable format (JSON export).
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — at any time, where processing is based on consent, without affecting the lawfulness of past processing.
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your country of residence.

To exercise any of these rights, write to support@healthmaster.app. We will respond within 30 days. The legal bases on which we process data are: performance of a contract (to deliver the service you signed up for), legitimate interest (security, fraud prevention, product improvement), consent (optional features such as notifications), and legal obligation (tax records).

11. California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information we collect, to request deletion of your personal information, to correct inaccurate information, and to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information. We do not sell personal information for money, and we do not share it for cross-context behavioural advertising. To exercise your CCPA rights, write to support@healthmaster.app with the subject "CCPA request". We do not discriminate against users who exercise their privacy rights.

12. Security

  • All traffic between your device and our servers is encrypted with TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Data at rest in our primary database is encrypted, and backups are encrypted before being uploaded to cold storage.
  • Access to production systems is restricted by role, audited, and protected by multi-factor authentication.
  • Photos and voice recordings are stored in object storage with private, time-limited signed URLs.
  • We monitor for anomalous activity and rate-limit suspicious requests.
  • In the event of a personal data breach likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours and notify affected users without undue delay, as required by Article 33 and Article 34 GDPR.
